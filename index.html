Free Tax Help - Westbank Regional - VITA

Free assistance filing your tax returns



The IRS's Volunteer Income Tax Assistance (VITA) and Tax Counseling for the Elderly (TCE) programs offer free basic tax return preparation to qualified individuals in partnership with CHEC (Community Housing Education Center).


Appointment Times: 4pm-6pm
Walk-In Times: 6pm-8pm


The VITA program has operated for over 50 years. VITA sites offer free tax help to people who need assistance in preparing their own tax returns.

The VITA team will be available year-round to help low- and medium-income individuals and families prepare and e-file their income tax returns.

This free tax preparation service provides filing assistance to qualified residents who:

  • Have income $67,000 or less
  • Have no rental property or farm income
  • Have Expenses $5,000 or less and no net loss
  • Have picture ID
  • Have Social Security Card(s)
  • Have (if applicable) Form 1095-A (Health Insurance Statement)
  • Have a copy of previous year’s tax return (if applicable)
To file taxes electronically on a married-filing-jointly tax return, both spouses must be present to sign the required forms.

ITEMS YOU MUST BRING

  • Driver’s license, state photo ID, or other government issued photo ID for you (and your spouse if filing a joint return)
  • Social Security cards or Individual Tax Identification Number (ITIN)
  • Date of Birth for you, your spouse, and all dependents
  • Wage and earning statements
  • Form W-2
  • 1099-NEC from all employers in 2024
  • Social Security year-end benefit statement
  • (SSA-1099) you received
  • 1099-R (retirement distribution statement) forms you received
  • Bank interest (1099-INT) forms you received
  • 1099-MISC (other income), 1099-G (government payments, including unemployment)
  • 1099-B (brokerage statements), and W-2G (gambling income)
  • If you had Healthcare.gov health insurance, your 1095-A form
  • Bank routing number and checking/savings account number
  • Last year’s state and federal tax return if you filed
  • IRS Identity Protection Personal Identification Number or IPIN, if you have one


ITEMS TO BRING IF THEY APPLY TO YOU

  • Proof of child care expenses paid (statement from provider with EIN# or Social Security #)
  • Tuition statements (1098T) for you, your spouse, or your dependents
  • Student loan interest statement (1098E)
  • If you are a K-12 teacher, total of unreimbursed classroom expenses
  • Mortgage interest statement (1098)
  • Property taxes paid in 2024 if they are not on your mortgage statement


IF YOU ARE SELF-EMPLOYED, INCLUDING DRIVING LYFT OR UBER

A list of income including tips and any 1099- NEC forms you received A list of your business expenses, totaled by category. Form TBD Business mileage totals

Out of pocket medical payments, including health insurance premiums, medical, dental, vision, hospital, pharmacy costs, and mileage to and from medical appointments.
